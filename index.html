Best dark sky sites in Southeast Asia?
General Discussion · 48 replies · 1,247 views · Pinned
NK
NightKing
⭐ Senior Member
Mar 15, 2026 · 09:14
Hey everyone! I've been planning an observing trip across Southeast Asia and want to find the best Bortle 1-2 dark sky sites. I'm particularly interested in sites accessible without 4WD, ideally with some accommodation nearby.

I'm from Singapore so I'm thinking Cameron Highlands in Malaysia, maybe some sites in northern Thailand (Doi Inthanon area?), or even further afield in Borneo.

Has anyone done any serious observing in this region? Monsoon season timing is also a concern — any advice appreciated! 🔭
👍 Like (34) 💬 Quote 🔖 Save
AP
AstroPaul_KL
🌟 Contributor
Mar 15, 2026 · 11:30
Cameron Highlands is genuinely excellent! Brinchang station area reaches Bortle 3 on good nights. The highland plateau cuts out much of the horizon light pollution. Best months are Feb–Apr and Jun–Aug (avoid NE monsoon).

For something more remote, Sabah (East Malaysian Borneo) has phenomenal skies. The Crocker Range area is particularly good. You need a local guide but accommodation is available in Tambunan.

Doi Inthanon is incredible — Thailand's highest peak and the national park has a dedicated observation area. I got Bortle 2 readings there. The astronomy club runs star parties on new moon weekends, worth joining. Avoid Nov–Jan for best skies (dry season).
